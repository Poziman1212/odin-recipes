How to make Tonkatsu

Description

We have a specialty in Japan known as Tonkatsu (とんかつ, 豚かつ) or deep-fried pork cutlet. This Western-inspired dish has been enjoyed for over 120 years, with the recipe remaining mostly unchanged since then. So, what makes Tonkatsu special, and how can we make this popular dish at home? Believe it or not, making Japanese pork cutlet at home is easier than you think. It should take no more than 30 minutes!

Ingredients

  • Pork
  • Salt and pepper
  • Flour
  • Egg
  • Panko
  • Neutral flavored oil for deep frying
  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • Optional Sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Cut the pork in half
  2. Dread the pork in the flour
  3. Dread the pork in the egg
  4. Dread the pork in the panko, make sure to dread it fully for more crisp
  5. Add 3 cups of neutral oil and boil it to 170C (340F)
  6. Add the pork covered in flour, egg, and panko in the oil. Do not move it around that much and fry until brown
  7. Bring the fried pork in a plate, then add some tonkatsu sauce and done!
